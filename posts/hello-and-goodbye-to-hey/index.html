Hello and Goodbye to Hey

2020-06-26

I was invited to Basecamp’s Hey email beta on June 16, and finally got around to playing with it this week. I’m not seeing what the major upgrades are, particularly when (after a two-week trial) Basecamp are asking $99/year for Hey.

The iOS client is neatly designed and has well-written copy. The introductory emails are sensibly put together. But, the highlighted workflow features are a minor rebranding of similar features commonly available on free email services. To compare to the obvious competitor - Gmail:

  • “Imbox” is an awkward title for any email’s Inbox, with some elements of Gmail’s Priority Inbox.
  • “The Feed” is Gmail’s “Promotions” category.
  • “Paper Trail” is Gmail’s “Updates” category.
  • “Set Aside” is the archive function of any email service.
  • “Reply Later” is the “remind me to address this later” option of any modern email offering.

I could potentially recommend Hey to someone who struggles to create their own email workflow, so would appreciate more a guiding hand. For my use: I wish the team well, but Iâ€™m about $95 short of seeing $99 of annual value.
